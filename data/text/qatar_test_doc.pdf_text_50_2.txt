Real Exchange Rate 
Background. The Riyal has been pegged to the U.S. dollar 
at 3.64 since July 2001. Qatar’s nominal effective exchange 
rate (NEER) appreciated by 1 percent, similar to the US’s. 
Qatar’s real effective exchange rate (REER) weakened by 
1 percent in 2023 as the nation’s inflation was below its 
trading partners’. In 2024 through November, REER 
depreciated somewhat from the same period in 2023.   
Assessment. With large reliance on hydrocarbon exports 
priced in U.S. dollars and elastic supply of expatriate labor, 
the impact of REER performance on Qatar’s
